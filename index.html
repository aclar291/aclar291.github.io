Let’s talk about it!

Picture this: it’s Friday night and you’re bored. You and your friends want to go out, but you’re tired of clubs and the random dances that people do in them. You want an organized yet fun dance setting that still plays the club music you know and love, and you’re wondering where to go. The solution is simple: go to line dancing!

What is line dancing?

Line dancing is an activity that involves a bit of learning, a bit of coordination, and a lot of fun, and it’s sweeping the nation. Held weekly at many different bars and dance halls, you could go out dancing every night of the week if you wanted to! Most spots will have a DJ who will play songs, and if you know the dance you hop onto the dance floor. You’re always encouraged to go on the dance floor if you don’t know a dance so that you can follow along and learn! There are usually one to two lessons a night where a dance instructor walks newcomers through the steps of a beginner level dance, but the best way to learn dances is by jumping straight in. Even if you don’t think you can dance, you can do it! It's that easy. All skill levels are welcome.

What should you bring to line dancing?

You’ll definitely want to bring:

  • a pair of comfortable shoes
  • stylish clothes you can dance in
  • an electric or hand-powered fan for when it gets hot
  • perfume or extra deodorant if you get stinky

A lot of people will wear cowboy boots to line dancing, but if they hurt your feet to dance in then don’t wear them! Sneakers work just fine. People tend to wear jeans while dancing, and on special occasions (like Halloween) they might wear costumes. It gets hot when you dance for hours on end, so bring a fan to cool off. If you know you might sweat a little and get stinky, always keep extra deodorant or perfume handy.
